SKIN DISEASE: PSORIASIS
========================

OVERVIEW:
Psoriasis is a chronic, immune-mediated inflammatory disease affecting skin and joints in approximately 2-3% of the global population (~125 million people). Characterized by accelerated keratinocyte proliferation (turnover reduced from 28 days to 3-4 days), leading to well-demarcated, erythematous plaques with silvery scales. Has significant systemic implications including psoriatic arthritis, cardiovascular disease, metabolic syndrome, and psychological comorbidities.

CLASSIFICATION:
- Plaque Psoriasis (Psoriasis Vulgaris): 80-90% of cases
- Guttate Psoriasis: Drop-shaped lesions, often triggered by streptococcal infection
- Inverse (Flexural) Psoriasis: Smooth, shiny plaques in body folds
- Pustular Psoriasis: 
  * Generalized (Von Zumbusch) - systemic, potentially life-threatening
  * Palmoplantar (PPP)
  * Acropustulosis
- Erythrodermic Psoriasis: >90% BSA involvement, medical emergency
- Nail Psoriasis: Pitting, onycholysis, oil-drop sign, subungual hyperkeratosis
- Psoriatic Arthritis: Affects 30% of psoriasis patients

SEVERITY ASSESSMENT:
- Mild: BSA <3%, PASI <10, DLQI <10
- Moderate: BSA 3-10%, PASI 10-20
- Severe: BSA >10%, PASI >20, DLQI >10

PATHOPHYSIOLOGY:
1. Genetic susceptibility - HLA-Cw6 allele (strongest genetic association), PSORS1-PSORS9 loci
2. Environmental trigger activates innate immunity
3. Plasmacytoid DCs produce IFN-α; myeloid DCs activate T cells
4. Th1/Th17 polarization: TNF-α, IL-17A, IL-17F, IL-23 axis is central
5. IL-17A and IL-22 drive keratinocyte hyperproliferation and barrier dysfunction
6. VEGF-mediated angiogenesis fuels inflammatory microenvironment

SYMPTOMS:
- Well-demarcated erythematous plaques with thick silvery-white scales
- Auspitz sign: pinpoint bleeding when scale removed
- Pruritus (present in 70-90%), burning sensation
- Koebner phenomenon: new lesions at sites of skin trauma
- Nail changes in 50% (pitting is pathognomonic)
- Joint pain/stiffness in psoriatic arthritis
- Geographical tongue, fissured tongue
- Quality of life significantly impacted

AFFECTED AREAS:
- Extensor surfaces: elbows, knees, shins
- Scalp (50% of cases)
- Lumbosacral area
- Umbilicus, intergluteal cleft
- Nails
- Palms and soles (palmoplantar psoriasis)

TRIGGERS:
- Streptococcal pharyngitis (guttate psoriasis)
- Stress and psychological factors
- Medications: beta-blockers, lithium, antimalarials, NSAIDs, ACE inhibitors
- Alcohol consumption and smoking
- Trauma to skin (Koebner phenomenon)
- HIV infection
- Obesity
- Winter season (lack of UV exposure)

DIAGNOSIS:
Primarily clinical. Histopathology when uncertain:
- Acanthosis with elongated rete ridges
- Parakeratosis with neutrophil collections (Munro microabscesses)
- Suprapapillary thinning
- Tortuous dilated vessels in dermal papillae

Investigations:
- Skin biopsy for atypical presentations
- Joint X-rays/"pencil-in-cup" deformity in psoriatic arthritis
- ESR, CRP, uric acid, RF (to distinguish from RA)
- LFTs, metabolic panel before systemic therapy

TREATMENT PROTOCOLS:

Topical Treatments (Mild Disease):
- TCS (superpotent on body, mild on face/flexures)
- Vitamin D analogues: calcipotriol, calcitriol, tacalcitol
- Calcipotriol/betamethasone combination (Taclonex/Enstilar foam) - most effective topical
- Tazarotene (retinoid)
- Coal tar preparations (keratolytic, antipruritic)
- Salicylic acid (keratolytic for thick plaques)
- Dithranol (anthralin) - reserve for resistant plaques

Phototherapy (Moderate Disease):
- Narrowband UVB (311-313nm) - first-line phototherapy
- PUVA (psoralen + UVA) - more potent, higher carcinogenic risk
- Excimer laser for localized plaques
- Targeted phototherapy

Systemic Treatments (Moderate-Severe):
Conventional:
- Methotrexate 7.5-25mg/week (gold standard)
- Cyclosporine 2.5-5mg/kg/day (rapid effect, short-term)
- Acitretin (retinoid) - especially for pustular/erythrodermic

Biologics:
- TNF-α inhibitors: etanercept, adalimumab, infliximab, certolizumab
- IL-12/23 inhibitor: ustekinumab
- IL-17A inhibitors: secukinumab, ixekizumab, bimekizumab
- IL-17RA inhibitor: brodalumab
- IL-23 inhibitors: guselkumab, risankizumab, tildrakizumab (newest, highest PASI 90/100 rates)

Oral Small Molecules:
- Apremilast (PDE4 inhibitor) - oral, well-tolerated
- Deucravacitinib (TYK2 inhibitor) - newer, selective
- Tofacitinib (JAK1/3) - for psoriatic arthritis

COMPLICATIONS:
- Psoriatic arthritis (can be debilitating, irreversible joint damage)
- Cardiovascular disease (2-3x increased risk MI)
- Metabolic syndrome, diabetes, obesity, NAFLD
- Inflammatory bowel disease (Crohn's, UC)
- Depression, anxiety, suicidal ideation
- Erythroderma and generalized pustular psoriasis (life-threatening)
- Uveitis, oral lesions

ICD-10 CODE: L40.0 (Psoriasis vulgaris), L40.9 (Psoriasis, unspecified)

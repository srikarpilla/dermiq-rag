SKIN DISEASE: MELANOMA & COMMON SKIN CANCERS
=============================================

OVERVIEW:
Skin cancer is the most common malignancy in fair-skinned populations. Three major types: melanoma (most dangerous), basal cell carcinoma (BCC), and squamous cell carcinoma (SCC). Early detection is critical — 5-year survival for localized melanoma is 99% vs 27% for metastatic disease.

SECTION A: MELANOMA
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
The malignant transformation of melanocytes. Accounts for only 1% of skin cancers but causes majority of skin cancer deaths.

Subtypes:
- Superficial spreading melanoma (70%): Most common, horizontal growth phase
- Nodular melanoma (15-30%): Vertical growth, aggressive, often amelanotic
- Lentigo maligna melanoma (10%): Elderly, sun-damaged skin, slow growing
- Acral lentiginous melanoma (2-8%): Palms, soles, nail apparatus; predominant in darker skin types

ABCDE Criteria for Recognition:
- Asymmetry: One half doesn't match the other
- Border: Irregular, ragged, notched, or blurred
- Color: Variation — shades of brown, black, red, white, or blue
- Diameter: Greater than 6mm (size of a pencil eraser)
- Evolving: Any change in size, shape, color, or new symptom (bleeding, crusting)

Additional warning sign: "Ugly Duckling" — lesion looks different from all other moles

Staging (AJCC 8th edition):
- Stage 0 (in situ): Confined to epidermis
- Stage I: ≤2mm thick, no ulceration or limited ulceration
- Stage II: >2mm or with ulceration, node-negative
- Stage III: Regional lymph node involvement
- Stage IV: Distant metastasis

Pathophysiology:
- UV radiation (UVA/UVB) causes DNA damage (C→T transitions at CC/TT dipyrimidines)
- BRAF V600E mutation: 50% of cutaneous melanomas
- NRAS mutations: 20%
- NF1 mutations: 15%
- KIT mutations: mucosal/acral/chronically sun-damaged

Treatment:
- Primary excision with wide local excision (WLE) margins based on Breslow thickness
- Sentinel lymph node biopsy for tumors >0.8mm
- Adjuvant therapy: pembrolizumab, nivolumab, dabrafenib+trametinib (BRAF-mutant)
- Metastatic: Anti-PD1 (pembrolizumab, nivolumab), CTLA4 (ipilimumab), BRAF+MEK inhibitors
- Talimogene laherparepvec (T-VEC) for unresectable Stage III/IV

SECTION B: BASAL CELL CARCINOMA (BCC)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Most common skin cancer (>4 million cases/year in US). Arises from basal cells of epidermis. Locally invasive, rarely metastasizes.

Subtypes:
- Nodular BCC (60%): Pearly papule with rolled border and telangiectasias
- Superficial BCC (30%): Pink-red patch, trunk/extremities
- Morpheaform/sclerosing BCC: Scar-like, ill-defined, most aggressive
- Pigmented BCC: Melanin deposits, may mimic melanoma

Risk factors: Cumulative UV exposure, fair skin, immunosuppression, PTCH1 mutations (Gorlin syndrome), arsenic exposure, radiation

Treatment:
- Mohs micrographic surgery (gold standard for high-risk, recurrent, cosmetically sensitive areas)
- Surgical excision with 4mm margins
- Electrodessication & curettage (low-risk superficial/nodular)
- Topical: imiquimod, 5-fluorouracil (superficial BCC)
- Photodynamic therapy (PDT)
- Hedgehog pathway inhibitors: vismodegib, sonidegib (locally advanced/metastatic)
- Cemiplimab (anti-PD1) for advanced BCC after hedgehog inhibitor failure

SECTION C: SQUAMOUS CELL CARCINOMA (SCC)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Second most common skin cancer. Arises from keratinocytes. Metastatic potential ~5% for cutaneous SCC (higher for mucosal/immunocompromised).

Precursor: Actinic keratosis (AK) — rough, scaly patch on sun-damaged skin; 1-5 per 1000 AKs/year risk of SCC transformation

Subtypes:
- Well-differentiated SCC: Firm, hyperkeratotic nodule
- Poorly differentiated: Rapidly growing, friable
- SCC in situ (Bowen's disease): Full-thickness epidermal dysplasia
- Keratoacanthoma: Rapidly growing, may regress spontaneously
- Verrucous carcinoma: Slow-growing, locally aggressive

High-Risk Features: >2cm diameter, >2mm depth, perineural invasion, location on ear/lip, poor differentiation, immunosuppression, rapid growth

Treatment:
- Mohs surgery (high-risk features, head/neck)
- Wide local excision (low-risk lesions)
- Radiotherapy (adjuvant or primary in inoperable cases)
- Cemiplimab, pembrolizumab (advanced/metastatic SCC)
- Adjuvant radiation for perineural invasion
- AK field therapy: cryotherapy, imiquimod, 5-FU, PDT, diclofenac

PREVENTION FOR ALL SKIN CANCERS:
- Broad-spectrum SPF 30-50+ sunscreen (UVA/UVB protection)
- Protective clothing, hats (UPF 50+)
- Avoid peak UV hours (10am-4pm)
- Avoid tanning beds
- Regular full-body skin examinations
- Vitamin D supplementation if sun-avoidant
- Annual dermatologist check for high-risk individuals

ICD-10 CODES: C43 (Melanoma), C44 (BCC/SCC)
